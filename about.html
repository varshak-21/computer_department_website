Vision & Mission

DBIT will be known to have an innovative, enjoyable and holistic learning environment that enhances individual success, the Don Bosco way. We seek to make DBIT the preferred choice of students, employers hiring engineering graduates and practitioners seeking further education.

We aim to provide service in the best tradition of Don Bosco, by seeking to develop entrepreneurial attitude and social commitment in every student we train. DBIT will make a contribution to society through its educative process in a diverse and stimulating learning environment wherein students, faculty and staff can strive and grow.

Department Infrastructure

The department focuses on student development through project activity. To do this, teachers motivate students to carry out project development right from their second year of engineering. The facilities available in all the laboratories can be accessed throughout the working hours of the department.

COMP Centre-1
A climate-controlled lab featuring approximately 20 high-performance PCs dedicated to programming and software development. It provides an optimized environment for students to master technical skills and industry-standard software.

Bosco Hall
A versatile, air-conditioned space equipped with modern projection systems. Bosco Hall serves as a quiet study area, a lecture space, and a collaborative hub for Computer Department meetings and technical practice.

COMP Centre-2
Our secondary computing facility is designed for hands-on technical training. This lab supports advanced coursework and provides students with the digital tools necessary for complex project work and research.

Seminar Hall
A premier space designed for knowledge sharing, guest lectures, and industry seminars. Featuring a high-capacity layout, it is the center for academic events, workshops, and student presentations.

Our Classrooms
Our classrooms are designed for focused learning, accommodating up to 70 students. Each room is equipped with advanced projection technology, enabling faculty to deliver interactive, media-rich lessons that bring complex study materials to life.
